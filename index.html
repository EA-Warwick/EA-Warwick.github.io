What is EA Warwick?

A community built around doing good, better

EA Warwick is part of a global movement called Effective Altruism — dedicated to using reason and evidence to find the most effective ways to help others. We help Warwick students think clearly about how to have a real impact. Learn more about Effective Altruism

Our core principles

  • 01We work together to think clearly and seek truth, because this helps us do more good.
  • 02Our resources are limited, so we prioritise the ways we can help most effectively.
  • 03We strive to help those that need it the most, regardless of whether they are similar to us or live near us in space or time.
  • 04We take actions that benefit many over actions that benefit a few.

Programmes

Structured learning for deeper impact

Each programme runs for 8 weeks, starting from Week 3 of term. All are free and student-led. No prior knowledge of EA is needed.

📖 Introductory Fellowship
8 weeks +

Learn and discuss EA ideas with a small group of like-minded students. The Intro Fellowship is a free 8-week programme with weekly readings and facilitated discussions exploring the most pressing challenges of our time — and how to act on them.

Fellows join a campus community of dedicated altruists and gain access to socials, co-working space, and a global network of people committed to doing as much good as possible.

Apply View syllabus
🤖 AI Safety Discussion Group
7 weeks · with Warwick AI +

Co-hosted with Warwick AI, this 7-week fellowship is your entry point to a career in AI safety, ethics, and governance. Choose between a technical or governance track, with weekly readings and small-group discussions.

After building the foundations, you'll have the opportunity to develop a project to launch your work in AI safety.

Apply
🌱 Alternative Proteins Fellowship
8 weeks +

Explore solutions to some of the most pressing challenges of our time — from food insecurity to environmental degradation and animal suffering. The fellowship examines plant-based foods, fermentation technologies, and cultivated meat.

A free, student-led programme combining self-paced learning with weekly sessions, running from Week 3 to Week 10.

Apply
✍️ Effective Thesis Accelerator
8 weeks · ~5 hrs/week +

Designed to take you from an initial thesis or research idea to a fully developed, high-impact proposal with a clear Theory of Change. Combines self-directed modules and worksheets with weekly group discussions.

You'll explore what makes research high-impact, map critical global problems, and identify clear pathways to change. Facilitators offer 1-1 feedback on your projects throughout.

Apply Learn More
📜 Effective Project Fellowship
8 weeks · ~5 hrs/week +

Learn how to do high-impact research and produce a small piece of work that can support applications to prestigious programmes like ERA Cambridge. The fellowship walks you through problem selection, finding the bottleneck, developing a Theory of Change, and executing the project.

This programme runs alongside the Effective Thesis Accelerator.

Apply
